SXSW 2002!!

Submitted by jen on Wed, 03/20/2002 - 04:00
in
  • Pleasures

I love SXSW. Love the people, love Austin, love the molé sauce and margaritas and queso. Love-love-love-love-love!

Here are a few photos I snapped with my "retro" camera that takes pictures on a canister of film. You can still find places that will turn the film into printed pictures. I felt bad for disappointing all those people who kept running behind my camera to see their photos right after they were taken. (It's interesting how quickly new technologies breed new behaviors.)


Kiss, kiss!
Who wouldn't want to be Evan at that moment? Kissers: Molly & Justin


Jazz hands!
First night in town, nobody could bear to leave anybody else's side, so we ended up with aparty of 40 for dinner. There were lots of photos taken, but I believe mine was the only one with expressive "jazz hands".


Happy
Ben, Derek & Heather at the So New Media soirée (which you are sure to miss if you take 39 friends to dinner).


Key note boys.
Jeff and Kevin Lynch chillin' late night at the Omni. What lovely fellows.


Maggie and Me.
Just when you think you've met everyone at SXSW, you meet someone new and terrific. Maggie Berry and Bryan Mason were among this year's finds. (How did I manage not to get a photo of Bryan?!)


How many people can you fit into a petite-suite at the San Jose?.
Nearly 20, it seems. There wasn't much happening Monday night, so I invited a gang of folks to hang out in my room and take advantage of the bar in the lobby. The San Jose is a jewel of a hotel and I don't want to stay anywhere else. That's Jason Fried of 37signals on the right. Also pictured: Maggie, Courtney, Lane, Jeff, Meg, Matt, Anil.


Same party, different corner. Stewart and Mike take it all in.


And how did you enjoy the mescal?
Justin, Peter, and Jeff discussing the finer points of Bruce Sterling's eclectic liquor cabinet.


While shopping on South Congress...
Peter sports the latest look in cassocks.


While shopping on Lamar...
Molly looks through rose-colored (and heart-shaped!) glasses.


The Sunshine Fix!
Note to self: If you spend the better part of a week joking with your friends how you are "stalking" your new favorite band, when you finally meet said band, you will probably sound like a stalker. Got to see the Sunshine Fix do a rare acoustic set before I left Austin. Bill Doss remembered my relentless self-promoting from their Cambridge show ("You're Jentown, right!"). Unfortunately, I missed all their "real" shows later that week.


Music buddy.
Harold has been on the same panel as me for the past two conferences. This time around, we discovered that we like all the same bands. He and his friend Tony made for good companions for the Music Festival. (As did Maura Johnson, another fine friend who somehow slipped by my shutter.)


The grand finale.
The last show of my last night in Austin just happened to be The Shins! If you know me, you know I'm crazy 'bout the Shins. They put on a great show and it was thrilling to see them play for such a huge crowd.

The next morning, my kind host, the fabulous Ben Brown gave me a lift to the airport. And thus ended SXSW2002.
